Husky Study Hub

Problem Description

Our project intends to connect students with study spots at UW Seattle. With more than a dozen libraries and even more buildings available to study in, UW has the perfect space for any given student. Whether you thrive in a collaborative or silent environment, and whether your taste leans toward a modern or traditional aesthetic, there is definitely a spot suitable for you.But given the universityâ€™s 700-acre campus, many of these study spots may be underutilized and/or unheard of. Students are generally concentrated in popular buildings like Odegaard, the HUB, Suzzallo and Allen. As great as they are, they fill up quickly whereas lesser known spots may have a plethora of space available. There is currently no resource that conveniently provides information on places to study for UW students. UW Scout is an existing attempt at informing students on study spots the campus has to offer. While it shares helpful information including business hours, resource availability, location, and reservation details, it focuses almost exclusively on libraries and lacks information on the abundance of other spots UW students are free to study in.
